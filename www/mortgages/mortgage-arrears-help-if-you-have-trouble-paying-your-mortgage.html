Mortgage Arrears Help

If you need help repaying your mortgage or find that you’re in account is in arrears, there are several options available to get you back on your feet. We understand that one’s financial circumstances can change month to month due to emergency expenses or a sudden drop income and so it might be difficult to keep up with monthly repayments. Fortunately there are ways to overcome mortgage arrears and below we discuss the best ways to repay your mortgage including government schemes, insurance and speaking to your mortgage provider.

Contact your mortgage provider

It is important that you contact your mortgage provider before you realize that you cannot pay or you may be charged additional fees for your account going into arrears. By speaking to your lender, they should be able to offer your ‘forbearance’ and this means that they can offer ways to help make the repayments more manageable.

Lenders can reduce the cost of your mortgage by offering lower monthly repayments or allow you to defer repayment for a month also known as a ‘mortgage payment holiday.’ If you have previously overpaid on your mortgage, you may be on track overall and therefore the lender may allow you to take a break from your monthly repayment schedule.

Reduce the cost of your mortgage by switching repayment options

Mortgages are portable meaning that borrowers can change the type of mortgage they have to make the repayment options more flexible. For example, you can switch your current mortgage to an interest only mortgage, which means that you will pay lower monthly repayments by only paying the interest and leaving the capital to be repaid at the end of the mortgage term.

An additional option is to ask your lender to extend the mortgage term. If your loan currently lasts for 25 years, you can ask the lender to extend the mortgage for 40 years. This will reduce the monthly repayments in the short term and help if your account is currently in arrears. However, extending the length of the mortgage will mean that the loan is open for longer and therefore will increase the overall cost.

Sell your home and rent it out

If you don’t think that your financial situation will improve in the near future, you could sell your property or rent it out to the public to recover your debt. This is seen as a better alternative to incurring further arrears charges or potential repossession of your home.

Mortgage insurance

Mortgage Payment Protection Insurance (MPPI) is available to cover the cost of your mortgage should you have an accident, fall ill or become unemployed as a result. MPPI is available to purchase in the early stages of your mortgage and it is worth checking your mortgage agreement to see if you already have this. MPPI can also acquired during the mortgage term but you are less likely to be eligible if you are already injured, sick or unemployed.

Individuals will not be able to claim this type if currently fit, healthy and working. Therefore, if you are struggling with your mortgage payments but are still employed, it will not help reduce the cost of your monthly repayments.

Government schemes to help repay your mortgage

Support for Mortgage Interest (SMI) means the government will pay only the interest of your loan and not the capital. The government can offer help with the first £200,000 of your outstanding mortgage until you are in a financial position to repay. To be eligible, individuals must be on income support such as job seekers’ allowance (JSA).

The Mortgage to Shared Equity Scheme is available for those that have experienced payment shocks such as a drastic change in their income. With this scheme, borrowers are able to sell part of their property to the government to raise funds and they will have the option to buy it back later on. It is available to those that have been in mortgage arrears for over 3 months, own at least 25% of their property and have less than £2,000 worth of savings. The value of your house will be taken into consideration and must be above a certain level.

Individuals may be eligible for other schemes based on their criteria and location. For more information, visit the Citizens Advice Bureau Mortgages Arrears Guide.

Create a budget to manage your mortgage repayments

One of the simplest ways to manage your mortgage your repayments is to put together a budget and prioritize all your debts. Start by devising a list of your outgoings and try to cut out the non-essentials such as holidays, taxis, coffee and smoking. There are several ways to decrease your monthly home costs by switching providers for broadband, heating, gas and electricity. Using comparison sites is a great way to save on your utility and supermarket bills. You can use our own comparison tables if you want to get better rates on savings accounts and credit cards.

Do not apply for other loans

If you are struggling to make your mortgage repayments, customers are advised not to apply for further loans to repay the outstanding balance. Taking out other personal loans will simply delay the repayment and may lead to a spiral of debt. Furthermore, applying for a number of additional loans may negatively impact one’s credit score as it suggests that the individual is financially stretched and desperate for finance. This may worsen the individual’s credit rating further if they have fallen into arrears from missing their mortgage repayments.

If you want to speak to a professional about your debt, you can get free debt advice from The Money Advice Service.
