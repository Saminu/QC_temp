Reasons you could have been declined

If you are declined it is with good reason, applying again with the same details is not likely to result in a successful application.

What we recommend you do is space your applications out by at least 5 minutes and try applying for different types of credit. Apply for a smaller amount, use a guarantor or logbook loan, there are many options which you can get accepted for.

Please have a scan over the topics below and use the services we have provided.

No Credit History

If you have never had any credit before, lenders are hesitant to lend to you as they have no history of payments to determine how reliable you are. An appropriate loan is one tailored for newcomers or for poor credit ratings.

We suggest a credit building credit card, it is the fastest way to improve your credit rating and you can use the credit on the card to cover the costs which you need a loan for.

Bad Credit History

If you have a bad credit history, lenders will assume a similar scenario will occur again if they lend to you. Lenders will likely limit how much they will lend to you, to see if you can be responsible with smaller amounts. This means you will need to build your credit rating back up to be able to acquire larger sums of credit.

We suggest a guarantor or logbook loan, having someone to apply with you or a car as capital reduces the risk immensely.

Too Many Active Loans / Existing Debt

When the lender looks up your credit history they will see every form of credit you have taken and your track record of payments. Active loans and recent missed payments is a big warning for lenders which makes you a riskier customer.

We suggest a debt consolidation loan to combine all your various debts into one, making repayments easy.

Change Jobs Frequently

Lenders like consistency in your financial situation, it allows them to calculate your repayments based off your earnings. If you change jobs often they must consider your job changing or you becoming unemployed for a period of time.

Too Many Existing Credit Cards

If you have unused credit cards active, lenders may be hesitant to lend to you as you already have a sum of credit you can use. Either make use of that credit or cancel those cards.

Not Registered on the Electoral Roll

It is a requirement if you require any form of credit like a loan, credit card or even a mobile phone contract you must be registered. Lenders cross reference your information you supply in your application, with your information on the electoral roll to confirm your identity.

f you're not registered, or even if you're just not sure, visit the AboutMyVote website to apply online or download your local authority's registration form. Print it, complete it and then simply send to the address at the top of the form. It takes about 3 minutes and you're done!

Credit Report

Your Credit Report is how you are viewed financially. It is used when making decisions on your credit application. Calculations are made on your Payment History, Amounts Owed, Length of Credit History, Types of Credit Used and New Credit.

Having mistakes or errors on your report or a poor score can lead to getting declined. Making sure your credit report is accurate and up to date is important, especially when you are applying for loans or other finance.

Logbook Loans

Logbook loans are popular for those with bad credit who may have been turned down for loans by traditional banks or lenders. Not only can you get accepted with poor credit but you may be eligible for a larger sum of money compared to any other loan type.

Logbook loans refer to borrowing up to £50,000 secured on your vehicle. Whether it's your car, motorbike or van, you are asked to supply the lender with a 'log book' (V5 registration document) or MOT certificate for security. Borrowers will be asked to sign a form known as the 'Bill of Sale', which means that the lender will own your vehicle on a temporary basis. You are still able to use the car as normal so long as you meet all the monthly loan repayments. However, if you fail to repay the loan on the dates agreed, the bill of sale means that the lender can repossess your car without a court order.

By providing your car as security, it gives the lender more confidence that they can recover the cost if the borrower is unable to meet repayment. Borrowers must be aware that failing to meet repayment will lead to the repossession of their car and additional charges in order to repurchase it. You can borrow up to £50,000 depending on the value of your motor vehicle and repay over 2 to 5 years in weekly or monthly installments. You can receive funds directly into your bank account in one lump sum on the same day or the same week depending on the lender.

Debt Consolidation

Debt Consolidation is used when you owe several different companies money and all their repayments are too much to handle. Combining all the debt into one place allows you to make affordable repayments, and makes repayment negotiations simple and easy as they is only one company to communicate with.

The interest you pay will be decreased and you will be able to close down other credit card and loan accounts which will improve your credit rating. With each payment on your consolidated loan lenders will see you are being responsible and will be more likely to lend to you in the future.
