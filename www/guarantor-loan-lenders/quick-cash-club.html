Quick Cash Club Guarantor Loans

Quick Cash Club offers guarantor loans ranging from £100 to £750 repaid over 6 to 24 months in equal monthly instalments. Set up in 2012, the West Yorkshire- based firm, offer a completely online application via desktop, mobile or tablet and offer same day funds to those approved on a working day before 4pm. As a direct lender, there are no upfront admin or broker fees charged to applicants. As a guarantor loan lender, customers must have a guarantor involved as part of their loan application. This is usually a family member or friend who you know well and can discuss your finances with. The role of the guarantor is to agree to cover the cost of the loan if the main borrower cannot afford to repay. Guarantor loans are popular for those with a bad credit history because having a guarantor with a good credit record increases your chances of being approved.

How Does The Application Work For Quick Cash Club?

The application is completely online and the borrower will fill in their basic details about themselves and their guarantor and choose how much they want to borrow and for how long. After a few checks, if the borrower is provisionally accepted, they will be sent a unique PIN code by SMS. They will give this code to the person that they want to be their guarantor who will enter this PIN online to complete the application.

Quick Cash Club will carry out further checks on both the borrower and the guarantor including credit checks and affordability. By understanding both credit histories, they can get an idea of how well both parties have paid other loans in the past and if they have any debt outstanding.

The affordability measures look at both levels of income to get an idea of how much they can afford to borrow and repay. Quick Cash Club will usually perform a phone call to the borrower and the guarantor to confirm some details about their loan and make it clear that the guarantor must cover repayment if the borrower defaults.

If the application is approved, the funds can be sent to the guarantor’s bank account on the same day. This is a security measure to ensure that there is actually a guarantor as part of the transaction who can decide to send the money to the borrower or change their mind and send it back to the lender.

Am I Eligible For A Loan With Quick Cash Club?

Quick Cash Club states that there is an eligibility requirement for the borrower and the guarantor. For the borrower, they must be 18 years or over, UK resident, not bankrupt or IVA and have a valid debit card in order to receive their loan and collect repayments. Borrowers will also be required to have a working mobile phone so that they can be contacted by Quick Cash Club at any time but also receive their PIN code by SMS.

To be a guarantor, this is usually a family member or friend of the borrower. The guarantor is not typically a husband, wife or life partner. Quick Cash Club requires guarantors to be over 21 years old, have a good credit history, minimum income of £800 and employed.

Unlike other guarantor lenders, Quick Cash Club accepts guarantors that are not homeowners but having good credit and owning a car or home will increase the chances of the loan being accepted. This is because good credit guarantors are more likely to be able to repay a loan and also less likely to leave the country if they have a car and home.

How Do Repayments Work For Quick Cash Club?

The repayments for a Quick Cash Club loan are made by the main borrower of the guarantor loan and if they default on repayment, the guarantor is required to step in and pay the monthly repayment or outstanding debt.

Here is a representative payment example: Borrow £500 over 12 months. Repay £82.60 a month. Total repayable £991.20. Interest Rate 150% (variable).

The repayments are collected in equal monthly instalments for up to 24 months from the borrower’s debit account. Using a process known as continuous payment authority, the borrower’s debit card is tokenized during the application and it allows Quick Cash Club to collect the amount owed to them each month from the customer’s bank account on the scheduled pay date. This ensures a smooth repayment process and usually an email and SMS is sent on the days leading up to each monthly repayment so there are no surprises for the borrower.

Failing to repay the loan on time will require Quick Cash Club to pass on the information to a credit reference agency such as Experian or Equifax. Therefore, missed repayments may negatively impact the credit score of both the borrower and the guarantor. However, if the borrower pays their loan on time, the information is still passed on and may improve the customer’s credit score.
