Implications of non-repayment

Guarantor loan repayments are made in monthly instalments and the lender will usually send you an email and an SMS reminder on the days leading up to repayment.

We appreciate that financial circumstances can change and you may not be able to repay your loan on the dates specified. However, failing to repay your loan on time may increase the cost of your repayment significantly so it is important to know the possible outcomes of non-repayment.

If you know well in advance that you are unable to make a repayment, it is essential that you contact the lender immediately who may be able to offer forbearance. Forbearance means that the lender can understand the circumstances that you are in and may decide to offer you some flexibility such as a longer term to repay the loan or a lower repayment amount.

Default fees and daily interest

Some lenders will charge you a default fee for missing an instalment. This is a one-off fee of up to £30 that is added to the monthly instalment that is due. If you continue to repay late, a default fee can be added every month.

If you miss repayment, lenders may continue to charge you the daily interest of your loan. Your loan is calculated on a daily interest rate that will continue to accumulate if you do not repay your loan. E.g if your repayment is 7 days late, you will be charged an extra 7 days worth of interest.

Some lenders will charge you both daily interest and a default fee whilst some lenders will just charge you the daily interest for failing to repay.

Arrangement or Pay Plan

In extreme cases, the lender may be able to offer you an arrangement or pay plan for your loan. This consists of a lower repayable amount or a longer time in order to settle your debts. However, a pay plan is not a ‘get out of jail card’ because it may include the daily interest and default fee on top of your loan and make the overall payment more expensive.

Impact to your credit score

Failing to repay on time may negatively impact your credit score. The information that you were unable to repay is fed back to the credit reference agency and will be noted on your credit file. This note will be visible on the credit checks that other companies may carry out and therefore make it harder to obtain credit elsewhere in the future.

For the guarantor, there will be no charge to their credit score if the borrower defaults and they have to pay on their behalf. However, if both parties fail to meet repayment, it will negatively affect both of their credit scores accordingly.

Lawyer letters

Guarantor lenders will always contact you if you miss a repayment. They will get in touch via email, SMS or phone to remind you of repayment and discuss your options. If, however, you and your guarantor fail to respond to any communication regarding repayment, you may receive lawyer letters asking you to hold up your end of the agreement. A lender will never repossess your car or home to cover the cost of repayment because a guarantor loan is unsecured.
